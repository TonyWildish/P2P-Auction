    • How to Play

    Connect

    Press the Connect button to connect to the TREX server and play in real-time against others!

    After connecting, if you are the only current player, connect a second player. This will make the game more interesting.

    The top line of text gives connection status info. It also gives you the connection delays. The first number is time to send a Trex data unit from the applet, and the second number is processing time at the Trex server (rarely more than 10ms) plus round trip delay. If the horizontal bars in the top right corner are red most of the time, your delay is too long, and you will probably find the game slow.

    Please press Reset before leaving this page: that is to ensure the connection with the server is broken cleanly, for everyone's good.

    The Game

    This is a dynamic auction game. 100 units of resource are on sale, and all connected players (applets) can place bids. The bidding goes on until an equilibrium is reached (an equilibrium is when 15 seconds elapse with no new bid by anyone). At that point, the server computes allocations for each player. The game starts again after a 5 seconds pause (15 and 5 seconds are the defaults, I may change them once in a while).

    Strategy

    Select a strategy from the pull down menu. An automated strategy called "Optimal" is selected by default. In Optimal, the bids are placed according to a strategy that maximizes a utility function (profit) based on a random valuation of the resource.

    Your valuation, which is indicated by the red horizontal line, is what you think the resource is worth. Press Reset to start the game with a new valuation.

    Bids

    A bid is for a certain quantity at a certain price, i.e. a point (q,p).
  • in Manual Play, place your bid by clicking with the mouse on the white surface: a red circle will indicate where you have placed it, the circle becomes a full red dot when the bid is successfully entered on the server
  • the blue dots show your opponents' bids
  • the magenta (purple) dot shows your allocation (what you would get) from the current profile of bids. The rule (formula) which computes your allocation from the bids is described this paper.
    • You can only get allocations on the white surface, which is constrained by (and changes according to) the following:
  • your budget (100 units of money). Eg, you can get at most for 100 units at a price of 1, or 20 units at a price of 5.
  • you can only get the leftovers from higher bidders; the player who bid for the highest price gets the qty s/he bid for; the next ranked player gets what they bid or what's left, whichever is smaller; etc. So during the bidding, the white surface changes to show what you can get given the current opponents' bids.

    • The yellow graph shows your utility (profit) for the current profile of bids.

    • Portability/Bugs

    This applet works on all hardware/OS/browser platforms supporting Java. Please send comments and bug reports. Currently known bugs:
  • Netscape-3.x (HP-UX, Windows) may have trouble loading some of the classes of the applet. If this happens, try this, or this, or this. If it does not work please let me know, and send me the exact error message in the Java console, as well as the full URL through which you reached the applet.
  • Netscape-3.x (Windows only), the bids dots may be mis-identified (your own bids may appear as blue instead of red)
    • Please send comments and bug reports. I am very very interested in hearing how the applet performs from different places on the Internet, and on different machines. nemo@ctr.columbia.edu
